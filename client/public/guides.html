Complete Tile Buying Guide

Understanding Tile Types

Ceramic tiles are made from clay and fired at lower temperatures, making them suitable for walls and light-traffic areas. Porcelain tiles are denser, fired at higher temperatures, and ideal for high-traffic floors and wet areas.

PEI Ratings Explained

  • PEI 1: Wall applications only
  • PEI 2: Light residential traffic
  • PEI 3: Moderate residential traffic
  • PEI 4: Heavy residential/light commercial
  • PEI 5: Heavy commercial traffic

Size and Layout Considerations

Larger tiles create fewer grout lines and a more seamless appearance, while smaller tiles offer more design flexibility and slip resistance. Consider your space size and desired aesthetic when selecting tile dimensions.

Stone & Slab Selection Guide

Natural vs Engineered Stone

Natural stone offers unique patterns and character but requires more maintenance. Engineered stone provides consistent appearance and enhanced durability with lower maintenance requirements.

Popular Stone Types

  • Granite: Durable, heat-resistant, requires sealing
  • Marble: Elegant appearance, susceptible to etching
  • Quartz: Non-porous, consistent patterns, low maintenance
  • Quartzite: Natural stone durability with unique patterns
